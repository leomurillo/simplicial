**Scope:** §5 Closed-Form Rotation via the Exponential Map
**Reviewer:** Reviewer X (formalism & logic)
**Date:** 2026-04-20

## Critical
- None.

## High
- **[F-H-1] Unsupported plane-basis claim and missing degenerate-case handling** — In §5.1, lines 303–307 assert that `{P_\perp, KP}` is an orthogonal basis of the rotation plane and that `KP` has the same simplicial length as `P_\perp`, but neither statement is proved at the point where the derivation uses it. The needed justifications are available from earlier material, but they are not stated: orthogonality should be derived from simplicial skew-adjointness (`GK = KG` plus `K^\top = -K`, Remark 3.2(1)) together with `P_\perp = -K^2P` and `K^3 = -K`; equality of lengths should be justified by restricting to `H_u = u^\perp \cap H`, where Corollary 3.4 gives `K^2 = -I` and hence `K` is an isometry/quarter-turn on that plane. There is also an unhandled edge case: if `P \in \mathrm{span}(u)`, then `P_\perp = 0` and `KP = 0`, so the displayed set is not a basis at all. Proposed fix: after line 303, add a short proof of `\langle KP, P_\perp\rangle_s = 0` and `\|KP\|_s = \|P_\perp\|_s`, and insert a sentence such as "If `P_\perp=0`, then `P'=P`; otherwise `\{P_\perp, KP\}` is an orthogonal basis of `H_u`."

- **[F-H-2] Lie-algebra framing is not rigorous as written** — In §5.2, lines 325–331, the manuscript says "the set of all skew-symmetric operators on `H` that annihilate `\mathbf{1}` forms a 3-dimensional Lie algebra," but this is formally ill-posed. As operators *on* `H`, the phrase "annihilate `\mathbf{1}`" is meaningless because `\mathbf{1}\notin H`; that qualifier only makes sense for the chosen `4\times4` lift on `\mathbb{R}^4`. Also, "skew-symmetric" is left ambiguous: here the natural notion is skew-adjointness with respect to the simplicial inner product on `H`, not merely unqualified ambient matrix skew-symmetry. The identification with `\mathfrak{so}(3)` is correct in substance, but it needs the explicit bridge that on `H` one has `G|_H = \tfrac43 I_H`, so simplicial skew-adjointness and ordinary skew-symmetry coincide after restriction. Proposed fix: replace lines 325–327 with something like: "Restricting to the zero-sum hyperplane `H`, the simplicially skew-adjoint endomorphisms of the 3-dimensional inner-product space `(H,\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle_s)` form the Lie algebra `\mathfrak{so}(H,\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle_s)\cong\mathfrak{so}(3)`. Our matrices `K(u)` are a distinguished `4\times4` lift of such endomorphisms; in that lift they also satisfy `K(u)\mathbf{1}=0`."

- **[F-H-3] Structural-role parenthetical misidentifies the projection terms** — In §5.3, line 347, the sentence assigning roles to `I`, `K`, `K^2` is wrong as stated. In the Rodrigues decomposition `R = I + \sin\theta\,K + (1-\cos\theta)\,K^2`, the parallel projector is `I + K^2`, not `I`; and the perpendicular projector is `-K^2`, not `K^2` itself. `I` is the identity on all of `H`, while `K` is the in-plane quarter-turn on `u^\perp \cap H` and annihilates the axis. Proposed fix: replace the parenthetical with "as identity, in-plane quarter-turn, and negative perpendicular projection respectively; hence `I+K^2` is the parallel projector and `-K^2` the perpendicular projector."

## Medium
- **[F-M-1] Operator scope is left ambiguous at the boxed definition** — In §5.1, lines 297–321, the derivation starts with `P\in H`, so the geometric argument is explicitly intrinsic to `H`. But line 319 boxes `R(u,\theta) := I + \sin\theta\,K(u) + (1-\cos\theta)K(u)^2` without saying whether this is being defined as an operator on `H` or as the formal `4\times4` lift on all of `\mathbb{R}^4`. Earlier sections support both viewpoints: §4 treats `R` as a gauge-compatible lift on `\mathbb{R}^4`, while the geometry of rotation lives on `H`. Proposed fix: add one sentence after line 319 clarifying: either "This defines the rotation operator on `H`," or "The same `4\times4` formula gives the gauge-compatible lift on `\mathbb{R}^4`, whose restriction to `H` is the geometric rotation."

- **[F-M-2] Positive-angle convention is only implicit at the key sign choice** — In line 307, `P_\perp' = \cos\theta\,P_\perp + \sin\theta\,KP` fixes the sign convention for positive rotation, but the sentence does not explicitly tie that sign to the orientation data already set in §2.5 line 168 and the Hodge-dual discussion in §3.4 lines 267–269. The convention is recoverable from the manuscript, but not locally explicit. Proposed fix: append a brief reminder such as "with positive `\theta` defined by the right-handed orientation fixed in §2.5, so that `K(u)` acts as the positive quarter-turn on `u^\perp\cap H`."

- **[F-M-3] "Unit skew operator" is undefined and should be made precise** — In line 327, the phrase "unit skew operators" is nonstandard and currently unexplained. The intended meaning appears to be that for unit zero-sum axes, `K(u)` is normalized so that its nonzero eigenvalues are `\pm i`, equivalently `K^3 = -K`, equivalently `K^2 = -I` on `u^\perp\cap H`. Proposed fix: replace "unit skew operators" with one of these precise characterizations.

- **[F-M-4] The `\mathrm{SO}(3)` claim is asserted rather than justified** — In lines 329–331, the text says `\exp(\theta K)` "maps into `\mathrm{SO}(3)`," but gives no argument. Once the Lie-algebra framing is repaired, the needed proof is short: skew-adjointness on `H` implies orthogonality of the exponential, and `\det(\exp X)=e^{\operatorname{tr}X}=1` for traceless skew `X`. Proposed fix: add one clause after line 331 giving this standard argument.

- **[F-M-5] The higher-dimensional forward reference overstates what §9 establishes** — Line 347 says the Lie-algebraic derivation "generalizes to higher dimensions by replacing `K^3=-K` with the appropriate analog (discussed in §9)," but §9.2 lines 525–527 explicitly say that the analog of `K^3=-K` "(or its failure)" and the existence of a closed form are deferred to future work. So the present text promises more than §9 actually delivers. Proposed fix: soften the sentence to something like: "The wedge-Hodge construction itself has a higher-dimensional analog (see §9), but the analog of `K^3=-K` and any comparable closed-form exponentiation are not established here."

## Low
- **[F-L-1] Equivalence subsection would benefit from a one-line formal statement** — §5.3, lines 345–347, is mathematically acceptable because both derivations do indeed land on the same formula, but the subsection would read more rigorously if it stated the conclusion in operator form: "Therefore, on `H`, `R(u,\theta)=\exp(\theta K(u))=I+\sin\theta\,K(u)+(1-\cos\theta)K(u)^2`." This is polish rather than a substantive defect.

## Summary
No theorem in §5 appears invalid, and the arithmetic in lines 311–315 and the power-series regrouping in lines 333–341 are correct. The blocking issues are instead formal: the geometric derivation uses unproved plane-geometry facts and ignores the axial degenerate case, the Lie-algebra framing is imprecise at a foundational point, and §5.3 contains a genuinely wrong structural-role description of the Rodrigues terms.
