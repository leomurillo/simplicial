## Critical
- **[F-C-1] Appendix B trace computation is incomplete and arithmetically wrong** — Appendix B Step 2 (lines 612–632) does not actually complete the computation of `tr(K^2)`: line 628 ends in an ellipsis, and the parenthetical on line 630 replaces a derivation with prose. More seriously, the stated combinatorial identity on lines 616–622 is incorrect: for the unscaled matrix `\tilde K(u)`, the twelve off-diagonal squares sum to `8·sum(u_i^2)`, not `6·sum(u_i^2)`, because the six distinct cyclic-difference entries are exactly the six pairwise differences, whose squares sum to `4·sum(u_i^2)` under `sum(u_i)=0`, and each appears twice. As written, Step 2 does not rigorously establish `tr(K^2)=-2`, hence does not prove `c=1` or Theorem 3.3. Proposed resolution: replace Step 2 with a fully written self-contained computation of `tr(\tilde K^2)=-8·sum(u_i^2)`, then `tr(K^2)=-(8/3)·sum(u_i^2)=-2` using `(4/3)sum(u_i^2)=1`.
- **[F-C-2] The unscaled cubic constant in §3.1 and Remark B.1 is wrong** — The main text claims on lines 205–209 that for the unscaled ansatz `\tilde K(u)` one has `\tilde K(u)^3 = -(3/4) \tilde K(u)`, and Remark B.1 repeats the same normalization claim on line 638. That constant is incompatible with the chosen scaling `K=(1/\sqrt 3)\tilde K`: if `\tilde K^3 = -(3/4) \tilde K`, then one gets `K^3 = -(1/4) K`, not `-K`. After correcting the trace computation, the unscaled constant is `3`, not `3/4`, so the correct relation is `\tilde K^3 = -3\tilde K` with spectrum `{0,0,±i\sqrt 3}`. Proposed resolution: correct line 205 and Remark B.1 accordingly, or avoid stating the unscaled cubic law until the derivation is written cleanly from the trace computation.

One must still show that `u^perp ∩ H = im K(u)` (or an equivalent projection statement), then use `K^3 = -K` to deduce that `K^2 = -I` on that 2-plane while `Ku = 0` on the axis. Proposed resolution: add a short proof: decompose `P = P_parallel + P_perp`, note `K(u)u = 0`, use simplicial skew-symmetry to show `im K(u) ⊆ u^perp ∩ H`, compare dimensions via rank 2, and conclude `K^2 P_perp = -P_perp`.

## Medium
- **[F-M-1] The kernel claim `K(u)u = 0` is correct but under-justified where it is used critically** — Appendix B Step 1 invokes `K(u)u = 0` on line 608 "by direct computation, using the antisymmetric pattern," but gives no computation. The claim is true: each row gives a three-term cyclic cancellation, e.g. the first component is `(u_m - u_p)u_n + (u_p - u_n)u_m + (u_n - u_m)u_p = 0`. Proposed resolution: include one explicit row computation and state that the remaining rows are analogous.
- **[F-M-2] The minimal-polynomial step suppresses the diagonalizability input** — The explanation on line 240 and Step 3 on lines 634–636 jump from the spectrum of a real skew-symmetric matrix to its minimal polynomial without stating why Jordan blocks cannot occur. This is standard, but the formal reason is that real skew-symmetric matrices are normal (indeed orthogonally block-diagonalizable), hence diagonalizable over `C`. Proposed resolution: add one sentence citing the normal/skew-symmetric spectral theorem before concluding that the minimal polynomial is `x(x^2+c^2)`.
- **[F-M-3] Remark 3.2 mis-scopes simplicial skew-symmetry** — The remark says on line 232 that "for such u" (zero-sum unit axes) `K(u)` is skew-symmetric with respect to the simplicial inner product, whereas this property in fact follows from the entry pattern for every `u ∈ R^4`: all row and column sums vanish identically, so with `G = (4/3)I - (1/3)J` one has `JG = GJ` and in particular `GK(u) = (4/3)K(u)`, making `GK(u)` antisymmetric whenever `K(u)^T = -K(u)`. The restriction to zero-sum unit axes is needed for the cubic identity and normalization, not for metric skewness. Proposed resolution: separate the scope statements, reserving the zero-sum-unit restriction for `K^3 = -K` and unit-axis geometry.
- **[F-M-4] The Hodge-dual identification needs metric precision** — Section 3.4 states on line 258 "For V = H ≅ R^3," but the manuscript elsewhere distinguishes the simplicial inner product from the raw coordinate hyperplane and notes the 4/3 scaling in §2.5. The Hodge star depends on the chosen inner product, so the identification should be phrased as one of oriented inner-product spaces, not merely vector spaces. Proposed resolution: write that `(H, <·,·>)` is a 3-dimensional oriented inner-product space and hence non-canonically isometric to ordinary `R^3`, under which `K(u)` is identified with `*(u ∧ ·)`.

## Low

This is minor, but it obscures what the appendix is actually proving. Proposed resolution: either compute the characteristic polynomial explicitly in Appendix B, or change the phrase in §3.1 to "spectral/trace computation."
