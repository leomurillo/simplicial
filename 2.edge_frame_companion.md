# Companion Notes — The Edge-Axis Frame, A_4 = V_4 ⋊ C_3, and Janus as ε_{1234}

These notes are a follow-on to `fourier_circulant_rodrigues_notes.md`. They take the observation in Section 10 of that document — that the three opposite-edge axes of the tetrahedron are simultaneously circulant Rodrigues axes — and develop the structural consequences:

- the three opposite-edge axes form an orthonormal basis of `H_4` in the simplicial inner product,
- the four vertex axes are the four cube corners with sign product `+1` in that basis (one tetrahedron of the stella octangula),
- the two families of "special" rotations are the spectral incarnations of the semidirect-product decomposition `A_4 = V_4 ⋊ C_3`,
- the K(u) operator restricted to `H_4` is the standard `so(3)` generator against the simplicial metric,
- explicit bracket computation locates the chirality of K in a single bit: the orientation of the 4-index volume form on `H_4`,
- this bit IS the Janus polarity Andy's rotor paper isolates inside the Hamilton product.

The result is a tightening of both the K(u) paper and the rotor paper, replacing scattered "up to chirality" caveats with a single declared orientation axiom.

## 1. The Edge-Axis Frame as Orthonormal Basis of H_4

Take the three opposite-edge axes in zero-sum simplicial coordinates:

```text
e1 = u_AB|CD = ( s,  s, -s, -s)
e2 = u_AC|BD = ( s, -s,  s, -s)
e3 = u_AD|BC = ( s, -s, -s,  s)

s = sqrt(3)/4.
```

Each is zero-sum and unit in the simplicial norm `||x||_s^2 = (4/3) sum_i x_i^2`.

The pairwise simplicial inner products vanish by alternating-sign cancellation:

```text
<e1, e2>_s = (4/3) s^2 ( 1 - 1 - 1 + 1) = 0
<e1, e3>_s = (4/3) s^2 ( 1 - 1 + 1 - 1) = 0
<e2, e3>_s = (4/3) s^2 ( 1 + 1 - 1 - 1) = 0.
```

Thus `(e1, e2, e3)` is an orthonormal basis of `H_4` under the simplicial inner product. A Cartesian 3-frame sitting natively inside the four-vertex zero-sum hyperplane, with no gauge slack.

This is the "hidden Cartesian frame" of the simplicial system: not an extrinsic coordinate choice, but the intrinsic orthogonal frame the C_2 axes of the tetrahedral group lay down for free.

## 2. Vertex Axes in the Edge Basis: The Stella Octangula Realization

The four vertex axes (zero-sum representatives) expand cleanly in the edge basis:

```text
u_A = ( e1 + e2 + e3) / sqrt(3)
u_B = ( e1 - e2 - e3) / sqrt(3)
u_C = (-e1 + e2 - e3) / sqrt(3)
u_D = (-e1 - e2 + e3) / sqrt(3).
```

Verification, e.g. for u_A:

```text
(e1 + e2 + e3) / sqrt(3)
  = (1/sqrt(3)) (s+s+s, s-s-s, -s+s-s, -s-s+s)
  = (1/sqrt(3)) (3s, -s, -s, -s)
  = (3/4, -1/4, -1/4, -1/4)
  = u_A.
```

The other three follow by the same computation.

Each vertex axis is a unit vector in `H_4` whose coordinates in the edge basis are signs `(+/-, +/-, +/-) / sqrt(3)`. The four vertex axes have edge-basis sign patterns:

```text
u_A: (+, +, +)    sign product = +
u_B: (+, -, -)    sign product = +
u_C: (-, +, -)    sign product = +
u_D: (-, -, +)    sign product = +.
```

These are exactly the four corners of a cube whose three edges are aligned with `(e1, e2, e3)`, picked out by the constraint `sign product = +`. The complementary four cube corners have sign product `-`:

```text
(-, -, -), (-, +, +), (+, -, +), (+, +, -).
```

These are the vertices of the *complementary* (inverted) tetrahedron.

Together, the eight cube corners form the stella octangula — Kepler's two-tetrahedron compound. The opposite-edge frame `(e1, e2, e3)` is precisely the orthogonal axis frame of the circumscribing cube.

So the simplicial `N = 4` system contains the cube-frame intrinsically. This is a Synergetic fact — the IVM relations between tetrahedron, cube, and stella octangula make this transparent — but the Fourier-circulant analysis adds the spectral reason: `(e1, e2, e3)` is the simultaneous-diagonalization basis for the full V_4 subgroup of the tetrahedral rotation group.

## 3. Group Structure: A_4 = V_4 ⋊ C_3 and the Two Families

The tetrahedral rotation group `T` is isomorphic to `A_4`, the alternating group on four letters. It has 12 elements:

```text
1 identity
8 order-3 rotations  (about the 4 vertex axes, +/- 120 deg)
3 order-2 rotations  (about the 3 opposite-edge axes, 180 deg).
```

The three order-2 rotations plus identity form the Klein four-group `V_4`. Multiplication of double transpositions in `S_4` gives:

```text
(AB)(CD) o (AC)(BD) = (AD)(BC)
(AC)(BD) o (AD)(BC) = (AB)(CD)
(AD)(BC) o (AB)(CD) = (AC)(BD).
```

`V_4` is normal in `A_4`, with quotient cyclic of order 3:

```text
A_4 / V_4 = C_3.
```

Therefore:

```text
A_4 = V_4 semidirect-product C_3.
```

The two families of "special" rotations identified in the Fourier-circulant notes are exactly the Fourier shadows of this decomposition:

```text
3 opposite-edge axes -> generators of V_4
                      -> alternating Nyquist mode of the active 4-cycle
                      -> q_k = (1/4)(-1)^k + (1/2) cos(theta + 2*pi*k/4)

4 vertex axes        -> generators of C_3
                      -> m=1 Fourier triple on the active BCD 3-cycle
                      -> F, G, H = 1/3 + (2/3) cos(theta + 2*pi*k/3).
```

The action of C_3 on V_4 inside `A_4` is by conjugation: a 120-degree vertex rotation cyclically permutes the three opposite-edge axes. So `F, G, H` is literally the spectral expression of `C_3` acting on `V_4`.

This is the unification: the two "special" families are not parallel curiosities. They are the V_4 and C_3 factors of `A_4`, each read in its own Fourier basis. The cyclic structure that makes K(u) circulant in each case is the cyclic structure of the corresponding factor.

## 4. Lie Algebra: K|_H_4 is so(3)

The K(u) paper proves, for unit zero-sum u:

```text
K(u)^2 = -Pi_4 + (4/3) u u^T.
```

Restricted to `H_4`:

- `Pi_4 |_{H_4} = I_{H_4}` (the projector onto `H_4` is the identity there),
- `(4/3) u u^T |_{H_4} = P_u`, the orthogonal projector onto `span(u)` in the simplicial metric (because `||u||_s = 1` is exactly the normalization that makes `(4/3) u u^T` a unit-norm rank-1 projector under `<.,.>_s`).

Hence on `H_4`:

```text
K(u)^2 = -(I_{H_4} - P_u) = -P_{u^perp}.
```

This is exactly the standard `so(3)` identity for unit `u`: `K(u)^2 = u u^T - I = -(I - u u^T)`. The simplicial K(u) restricted to `H_4` is `so(3)` realized in 4D simplicial coordinates against the simplicial metric.

Consequence: the bracket `[K(u), K(v)]` for orthonormal zero-sum `u, v` lands on `K(u x_s v)`, where `x_s` is the simplicial cross product on `H_4` with respect to the metric and a chosen orientation. For `u, v, w` orthonormal, this gives:

```text
[K(u), K(v)] = +/- K(w),
```

with sign determined by orientation. No unexpected coefficient — the K(u) normalization is calibrated so the structure constants are `+/- 1`.

## 5. Explicit Brackets and Chirality of the Edge Frame

Direct computation with the K(u) entries from the paper, for the ordered edge basis `(e1, e2, e3)` and the paper's convention `epsilon_{ABCD} = +1`:

```text
[K(e1), K(e2)] = -K(e3)
[K(e2), K(e3)] = -K(e1)
[K(e3), K(e1)] = -K(e2).
```

So the ordered edge frame `(e1, e2, e3)` is *left-handed* with respect to the K orientation set by `epsilon_{ABCD} = +1`. The bracket structure constants are `-1`.

The complementary tetrahedron has natural edge basis:

```text
e1' = -e1
e2' = -e2
e3' = -e3.
```

By linearity of K:

```text
K(ei') = -K(ei).
```

Then:

```text
[K(e1'), K(e2')] = [-K(e1), -K(e2)] = [K(e1), K(e2)] = -K(e3) = +K(e3').
```

So:

```text
sign-product + tetrahedron:  [K(e1), K(e2)] = -K(e3)   left-handed
sign-product - tetrahedron:  [K(e1), K(e2)] = +K(e3)   right-handed.
```

The two regular tetrahedra inscribed in the cube give opposite-handed `so(3)` triples on the *same* edge frame. Janus polarity at the level of the Lie algebra is a single sign on the structure constants.

## 6. Janus Polarity = Orientation of the Volume Form on H_4

The K(u) entries are linear combinations of differences `(u_k - u_l)` weighted by signs. Equivalently:

```text
K(u)_{ij} = (1/sqrt(3)) * epsilon_{ijkl} * (u_k - u_l),
```

where `{k, l} = {1,2,3,4} \ {i,j}` and `epsilon_{ijkl}` is the 4-index Levi-Civita symbol normalized by `epsilon_{ABCD} = epsilon_{1234} = +1`.

Flipping the sign convention `epsilon_{1234} = +1 -> -1` sends:

```text
K(u) -> -K(u)
```

uniformly for every axis `u`. This is exactly the bracket-sign flip derived in Section 5. The two phenomena are the same single bit:

- the choice of orientation on the 4-vertex set (which permutations of `(A, B, C, D)` are "even"),
- the choice of which tetrahedron of the stella octangula is the "primary" one,
- the choice of left-vs-right circulant for `F, G, H`,
- the sign of the rotation angle `theta` for a given axis,
- the sign of the so(3) structure constants on the edge frame.

All of these collapse to one declaration:

```text
epsilon_{1234} = +1.
```

This is where Janus polarity actually lives. It is upstream of K, upstream of the Hamilton product, upstream of any rotor multiplication law. It is the orientation of the volume form on `H_4`.

## 7. Hodge / Wedge Restatement

The bracket structure is dual to the wedge structure on the edge frame. With `(e1, e2, e3)` as an orthonormal basis of `H_4` and `vol = e1 ∧ e2 ∧ e3` as the chosen volume form:

```text
e1 ∧ e2 = +(*e3)
e2 ∧ e3 = +(*e1)
e3 ∧ e1 = +(*e2)
```

with the Hodge star `*` defined by `vol`. The bracket sign is then:

```text
[K(ei), K(ej)] = sign(vol) * K(*( ei ∧ ej )).
```

Flipping `vol -> -vol` flips both the wedge orientation and the bracket sign in the same step. One sign in three costumes:

```text
volume form orientation
  = Hodge star sign
  = so(3) structure constants
  = Janus polarity tag.
```

This is the cleanest statement. The chirality of the Quadray system is a property of the volume form on the zero-sum hyperplane, not of any multiplication law built on top.

## 8. Edge-Basis Rodrigues Formula

Because `(e1, e2, e3)` is an orthonormal basis of `H_4`, every zero-sum unit axis decomposes as:

```text
u = alpha e1 + beta e2 + gamma e3,    alpha^2 + beta^2 + gamma^2 = 1.
```

By linearity of K:

```text
K(u) = alpha K(e1) + beta K(e2) + gamma K(e3).
```

The Rodrigues rotation becomes an edge-basis formula end-to-end:

```text
R(u, theta)
=
J_4/4
+ cos(theta) (I - J_4/4)
+ (1 - cos(theta)) (4/3) u u^T
+ sin(theta) [alpha K(e1) + beta K(e2) + gamma K(e3)].
```

No vertex coordinates are required at any step. The three matrices `K(e1), K(e2), K(e3)` are the canonical generators; `u` enters only through three real parameters `(alpha, beta, gamma)` on the sphere `S^2`.

This is the simplicial analog of the standard SO(3) Rodrigues formula written in a chosen Cartesian frame. The edge frame plays the role of `(i, j, k)`.

## 9. Recommendation for Leo's K(u) Paper

### Promote the orientation choice to an explicit axiom

The paper currently lets orientation propagate quietly through the worked examples. Many statements carry implicit "up to chirality" caveats. The structural source of all of them is one bit:

```text
epsilon_{1234} = +1.
```

Recommendation: open the paper with this declaration as an axiom. Then the following statements become unambiguous and the chirality caveats drop:

- the K(u) entries are uniquely determined,
- the bracket signs on the edge frame are determined,
- the F-vs-H ordering is determined,
- the choice of stella-octangula tetrahedron is determined,
- the right-vs-left circulant convention is determined.

Every later orientation-dependent statement is then a corollary, not a separate convention.

The only orientation-independent statement in the paper is `K(u)^3 = -K(u)`, which survives the flip because squaring kills the sign. Everything else inherits from `epsilon_{1234}`.

### Add the edge-frame as a canonical second basis

Currently the paper presents K(u) as an arbitrary-axis operator with the four vertex axes as the natural finite family. The orthonormal edge frame `(e1, e2, e3)` deserves equal billing because:

- it is a complete orthonormal basis of `H_4` (the four vertex axes are not — they are overcomplete by one because of gauge),
- the Lie bracket closes on it with structure constants `+/- 1`,
- it diagonalizes `V_4`,
- it is the cube-frame of the circumscribing stella octangula.

Recommended subsection: "The Opposite-Edge Frame and the so(3) Structure on H_4."

### Add the unification statement

The two families of special circulant rotations are not separate accidents. They are the V_4 and C_3 factors of `A_4 = V_4 ⋊ C_3`, each read in its own Fourier basis. Recommended remark, possibly at the end of the worked-examples section:

```text
The vertex-axis F,G,H rotations and the edge-axis alternating-mode rotations
are the spectral incarnations of the C_3 and V_4 factors of the tetrahedral
rotation group A_4 = V_4 semidirect-product C_3. The vertex rotations act on
the edge frame by cyclic permutation of (e1, e2, e3); the edge rotations are
the half-turns generating V_4. These are the only two cyclic relabeling
families compatible with the simplicial structure on N=4.
```

### Add the edge-basis Rodrigues form

The formula in Section 8 of these notes is a clean, vertex-free expression. It belongs alongside the existing `K(u) + Rodrigues` arbitrary-axis formula. Both forms are useful:

- the original is most natural when the axis is given in vertex coordinates,
- the edge-basis form is most natural for compositions, brackets, and Lie-algebraic reasoning.

## 10. Recommendation for Andy's Rotor Paper

### Collapse the Janus polarity into a single sign tag

The current treatment uses parallel "left" and "right" rotor variants with separate Hamilton-product rules. These are two specializations of one rotor algebra differing only by the orientation bit `epsilon_{1234}`.

Recommendation: introduce a single rotor type with an explicit polarity tag `eta in {+1, -1}` that records the chosen orientation. The Hamilton product rule then takes the form:

```text
rotor product with polarity eta
  = base product + eta * (orientation-sensitive cross term).
```

This collapses two parallel formula sets into one parametrized family. The Janus flip becomes `eta -> -eta`, applied uniformly. The reader sees in one declaration that left and right are not two different algebras — they are one algebra with its orientation bit toggled.

### Locate Janus geometrically, not algebraically

The current paper presents Janus polarity as a property of the rotor multiplication. The Fourier-circulant analysis shows it is upstream of multiplication: it is the orientation of the volume form on `H_4`, equivalently the choice of which inscribed tetrahedron of the stella octangula is "primary."

Recommendation: open the rotor paper with this geometric statement of Janus, then derive the multiplication law from it. The algebraic "left vs right" then appears as a downstream consequence rather than a primitive distinction.

### Replace the "bunch of algebra and trig" derivation of F, G, H

The current derivation of F, G, H from Hamilton-product compositions is opaque. The Fourier reason is one sentence:

```text
F, G, H are the inverse discrete Fourier transform of the spectrum
1, exp(i theta), exp(-i theta) on the active 3-cycle BCD,
plus a 1/3 gauge-projector lift.
```

This explains in one move why `F + G + H = 1`, why `F^2 + G^2 + H^2 = 1`, why `FG + GH + HF = 0`, why 120 degrees gives a pure permutation, and why the values are rational at tetrahedral symmetry angles. Recommended replacement subsection: "Why F, G, H Have This Form: The 3-Cycle Fourier Spectrum."

### Add the edge-axis rotor as the V_4 companion to F, G, H

The rotor paper covers the vertex-axis (`F, G, H`) family thoroughly. The edge-axis family with first-row coefficients

```text
q_k = (1/4)(-1)^k + (1/2) cos(theta + 2*pi*k/4)
```

is the natural V_4 companion. Together they cover both finite families of cyclically-special tetrahedral rotations. A parallel "Edge-Axis Rotor" subsection would complete the symmetry.

## 11. Open Questions and Further Lines

### Higher N

The N=5 cyclic case has two independent Fourier rotation planes. Does it admit an "edge-frame" analog — an intrinsic orthonormal basis of `H_5` coming from a finite symmetry subgroup analogous to V_4 inside A_4? The answer should be no in the same form, because the alternating group on 5 letters `A_5` is simple (no normal proper subgroup beyond identity). This is the group-theoretic reason the N=4 unification is special.

If true, this is the cleanest explanation yet for why N=4 is the largest case where rotations are single-axis objects: it is the largest N for which `A_n` has a normal V_4-type subgroup that diagonalizes simultaneously with a cyclic complement.

### Connection to quaternions

The three matrices `K(e1), K(e2), K(e3)` form an `so(3)` triple. Exponentiating gives an SU(2) double cover and hence a quaternion algebra. Concretely:

```text
i_q ~ exp(theta K(e1) / 2)  evaluated symbolically
```

and similarly for `j_q, k_q`. The standard quaternion relations `i_q^2 = j_q^2 = k_q^2 = i_q j_q k_q = -1` should hold up to the orientation sign. If yes, this gives an intrinsic Quadray quaternion algebra in 4D simplicial coordinates without ever leaving `H_4`.

### Bivector / wedge formalism

The opposite-edge axes are dual to the three pairs of opposite edges of the tetrahedron. Each opposite-edge axis is geometrically the midpoint-to-midpoint vector of a pair of opposite edges. This is naturally a bivector quantity, suggesting that the cleanest formalism for the simplicial system is geometric algebra on `H_4` with `(e1, e2, e3)` as the orthonormal vector basis and `(e1 ∧ e2, e2 ∧ e3, e3 ∧ e1)` as the bivector basis dual to it.

The `K(u)` operator then has a one-line geometric-algebra definition:

```text
K(u) v = u ∧ v under the Hodge star,
```

which would unify the K(u) construction with the wedge-Hodge discussion already mentioned in Section 21 of the original notes.

### Simplicial cross product

The bracket relation `[K(u), K(v)] = sign(vol) K(u x_s v)` defines the simplicial cross product `x_s` on `H_4` implicitly. Worth writing it out explicitly:

```text
(u x_s v)_i = ?    in terms of (u_1,...,u_4), (v_1,...,v_4).
```

The answer should be a Levi-Civita-style formula with `epsilon_{ijkl}`, restricted to the zero-sum constraint. This is the missing companion formula to the existing K(u) entries.
